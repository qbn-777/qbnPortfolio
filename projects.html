Automating Workflow for MetaStructure study

In this project, I addressed inefficiencies in the research team's workflow, specifically in generating and visualizing thousands—if not millions—of honeycomb variations. Manually computing seed locations and modeling each structure was time-consuming and impractical.

To streamline the process, I developed a parametric modeling solution that:

1. Automates seed location calculations using a Python-based framework, designed for scalability and improved generation algorithms.

2. Generates 3D models programmatically, eliminating the need for manual drawing.

This solution significantly enhanced efficiency, enabling the team to focus on analysis rather than repetitive modeling tasks.

Autonomous 2D navigation

I have worked on building a navigation stack for the TurtleBot platform, integrating key ROS packages to enable autonomous navigation (collision avoidance, path planning). This project involved setting up the ROS Navigation Stack, which includes:

1. SLAM (Simultaneous Localization and Mapping) for mapping the environment

2. AMCL (Adaptive Monte Carlo Localization) for localization

3. Path Planning & Obstacle Avoidance: Utilizing DWA (Dynamic Window Approach) Planner and global planners to generate efficient movement paths while avoiding obstacles dynamically.

4. Robot Kinematics & Control: Configuring TF (Transform Frames) and costmaps to ensure smooth motion planning and execution.

Optimised Container Movement for Insect Farm

During my internship at a Optik consulting firm, I addressed a logistical challenge for an insect farm struggling to move containers efficiently. My team analysed the problem, evaluated existing alternatives, and identified that renting forklifts was neither cost-effective nor suitable for the farm's rough environment. We designed a custom solution—a lifting mechanism station paired with wheel sets—to streamline container movement. Our approach included motion analysis, modal simulations, and material studies to ensure a robust, economically viable solution.

Handcrafted Brushed DC Motor & Voltage Measurement Project

I built brushed DC motors entirely from scratch, crafting the stator, wrapping the coils, sourcing magnets, and assembling the core and base using repurposed materials like speaker magnets and toy car gearboxes.

This project showcases my resourcefulness and problem-solving skills, allowing me to apply fundamental engineering principles while creatively sourcing materials from discarded items.

Visual Servoing for UR5 Aura Board Tracking

Developed a visual servoing technique to accurately track an aura board's position—independent of the camera mount's distance on the UR5. The operation was simulated in Swift, and the Peter Corke Python library was used to model the robot's link motion. Additionally, the system was implemented externally using a control driver provided by colleagues.
